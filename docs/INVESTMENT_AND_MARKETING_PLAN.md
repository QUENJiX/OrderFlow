## How To Use This Document

This is not a shopping list. It is a spending filter.

Use it like this:

1. Before spending money, check whether the spend helps get seller proof.
2. If it does not help you contact, onboard, or retain sellers, delay it.
3. If a provider asks for money but no pilot seller needs that provider yet, delay it.
4. If a tool makes you feel more official but does not create real usage, delay it.

Raw truth:

- A domain does not validate the business.
- A trade license does not validate the business.
- Meta App Review does not validate the business.
- Paid ads do not validate the business.
- Real sellers using OrderFlow for real orders validates the business.

## 2. Launch Strategy

Do not start with heavy paid ads.

Start with founder-led selling because this is a trust-heavy B2B product for small merchants. The first buyers need handholding, not a fancy ad funnel.

Real launch sequence:

1. Build a list of 80 target Facebook shops.
2. Contact the first 30.
3. Talk to 15 sellers.
4. Onboard 3-5 shops manually.
5. Set up their first 5-10 products yourself.
6. Ask them to send OrderFlow links to real customers for 7 days.
7. Track real COD orders, seller complaints, and courier export usage.
8. Ask for payment only after they have seen real value.
9. Use the first case study to reach the next 50 shops.

If you cannot get 3-5 sellers to try manual links, Meta automation is not the next problem. Distribution is.

## 5. Break-Even Math

If monthly operating cost is around BDT 5,000:

```text
Break-even at BDT 1,500/month = about 4 paying shops
Break-even at BDT 2,500/month = about 2 paying shops
```

If monthly operating cost is around BDT 12,000:

```text
Break-even at BDT 1,500/month = about 8 paying shops
Break-even at BDT 2,500/month = about 5 paying shops
```

If monthly operating cost is around BDT 18,000:

```text
Break-even at BDT 1,500/month = about 12 paying shops
Break-even at BDT 2,500/month = about 8 paying shops
```

If monthly operating cost is around BDT 40,000 because we choose a heavy AI/dev stack too early:

```text
Break-even at BDT 1,500/month = about 27 paying shops
Break-even at BDT 2,500/month = about 16 paying shops
```

This is why we should avoid heavy subscriptions and big ad spend before we have merchant retention. For the first 30 days, the target is not to look "fully funded"; the target is to keep break-even low enough that 5-8 serious shops can support the tool.

## 13. Legal and Business Setup

Costs vary by city, business type, and whether you use a lawyer/agent.

### Trade License

Bangladesh Trade Portal/BIDA-linked startup process references trade license fees generally ranging from BDT 1,000-20,000 depending on business type and authority, with very small application fees on top.

Budget:

```text
Trade license: BDT 3,000 - 20,000
```

### Private Limited Company

Not mandatory for the earliest software pilot, but useful for payment gateway/courier/provider trust.

RJSC official fee schedules show that government filing/stamp/name-clearance costs depend on authorized capital and documents filed. A small company can have relatively low official fees, but practical cost rises when professional help, stamp papers, certified copies, tax/VAT setup, and follow-up work are included.

Treat incorporation as a later credibility/provider step, not a first-30-day validation requirement.

Practical budget if using professional help:

```text
Private limited setup: BDT 20,000 - 80,000+
```

Recommendation:

- Start with a trade license if speed matters.
- Incorporate later if payment/provider onboarding requires it or if you bring partners/investors.

## 20. When To Spend More Money

Spend more only when one of these is true:

1. At least 5 shops are using it for real orders.
2. At least 2 shops have paid.
3. Merchants ask repeatedly for payment gateway automation.
4. Steadfast/API booking is requested by pilot shops, or courier CSV becomes a real bottleneck.
5. You know your rough cost per paying merchant.

Do not spend heavily just because the product looks good.

## 23. Final Recommendation

Launch lean, and start validation manually before automation.

Do not pay for every API and merchant account before proving demand. The best first investment is focused merchant onboarding plus a manual order-link workflow that proves sellers will use and pay.

Recommended order:

1. Build a CRM of 80 target shops and contact the first 30.
2. Use one paid AI/dev subscription by default; add the second only if it clearly increases shipping speed.
3. Run seller calls and recruit 3-5 pilot shops.
4. Launch the manual COD pilot with product/order links and courier CSV fallback.
5. Capture real orders and convert at least 2 shops to paid or payment-committed.
6. Buy domain and set up email when pilots are active or a public link is needed.
7. Deploy production app with Supabase + Vercel when real usage justifies it.
8. Publish public privacy policy, terms, data deletion, and support pages before Meta submission.
9. Build Meta OAuth, webhook, Page connection, comment/message ingestion, automated replies, and fallback handling.
10. Prepare the exact Meta App Review test Page, test user, screencast, and reviewer notes.
11. Then decide whether SSLCOMMERZ, bKash PGW, Nagad, Steadfast API, or additional courier APIs deserve cash immediately.

The first 90 days should prove one thing:

> Active F-commerce sellers will pay monthly because OrderFlow BD captures COD orders, reduces manual order/courier work, and later automates repetitive Facebook inquiry handling.
